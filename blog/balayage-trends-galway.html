Colour Trends

The Balayage Looks We're Obsessed With This Season

Siobhán Murphy
Colour Director, The Hair Company
1 April 2026  ·  5 min read

Spring has arrived in Galway — and with it, the most exciting colour requests we've seen in years. As Colour Director at The Hair Company, I'm in the privileged position of seeing exactly what looks our clients are bringing through the door, scrolling through on Instagram, and asking for by name. And right now? Balayage is having a very beautiful moment.

Whether you're already a balayage convert or you're considering making the leap for the first time, this is your season. Here's my honest breakdown of the looks we're loving — and why they're perfect for Galway.

1. Honey Brunette Balayage

This is the one. If you came to me tomorrow and said "just do something gorgeous," I'd probably reach for this. Honey brunette balayage works by weaving warm, golden-brown tones through natural darker hair — the result is this incredible, glowing warmth that looks completely natural but somehow more beautiful than natural.

"It's the Galway version of sun-kissed hair. Since the sun doesn't exactly do all the work for us here, we'll take the credit."

The beauty of this technique is in the hand-painting. We work with your natural hair's movement and growth pattern, so the colour develops where light would naturally fall. The result grows out gracefully — you're not back in the chair every 8 weeks for a root touch-up. Many of my clients go 4–6 months between appointments.

Best for: Natural brunettes, chestnut, or mid-brown hair tones. Works across all lengths but is particularly stunning on longer hair where the dimension really shows.

2. Copper & Rust

Copper is back and it's not being subtle about it. We're talking rich, fiery rust tones with movement and depth. This is the balayage look for someone who wants to make an entrance.

What I love about copper balayage as opposed to a flat copper colour is that it has dimension — you still have depth at the roots with those gorgeous warm tones developing toward the mid-lengths and ends. It's not flat, and it's not harsh. It's like you were forged in an autumnal Galway sunset.

This look requires a skilled colourist who understands tonal balance — too warm and it edges toward brassy, too cool and you lose the magic. Getting that balance is where the artistry lives.

3. Barely-There Blonde Balayage

For our naturally lighter clients, this season's version of blonde balayage is all about looking effortlessly natural — like you just got back from a long holiday (we can dream). Think very fine, scattered pieces rather than chunky sections. Soft. Subtle. Lived-in.

The key here is restraint. We're not adding loads of colour — we're adding the right colour in the right places. Often I'll use a gloss toner at the end to bring everything together and add an incredible glass-like shine.

Maintenance Tips from the Salon

Balayage is one of the lower-maintenance colour techniques out there, but here's how to make your colour last as long as possible:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo — avoid anything with sulphates, which strip colour and moisture
  • Wash with cool water — hot water opens the hair cuticle and fades colour faster
  • Purple or brass-toning shampoo — use once a week to counteract any brassiness in lighter balayage
  • Olaplex No. 3 at home — this is non-negotiable if you've had any lightening work done
  • Heat protection, always — GHD and Dyson tools are kinder, but still use protection
  • Book a gloss toner top-up at 8–12 weeks to refresh shine and tone between full balayage appointments

Ready to Try Balayage?

I always recommend booking a colour consultation before your first balayage appointment. This gives us time to look at your natural hair, discuss your lifestyle (how much time do you want to spend on maintenance?), and plan a colour journey that's right for you rather than just copying a photo from Pinterest.

Bring those Pinterest photos, though. I love seeing them — they're such a useful way to communicate. Just know that I'll always be honest with you about what's achievable in one sitting versus what might need a longer colour journey.

If you're in Galway and curious about balayage, book a consultation with me — I'd love to talk colour with you.
